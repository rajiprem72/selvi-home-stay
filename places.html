Places to Visit in Rameshwaram

Ramanathaswamy Temple

Popular Pilgrimage Destination

Built over several centuries by the southern rulers, the magnificent Ramanathaswamy Temple stands as a testimony to Tamil architecture and devotion. Famous for having the world’s longest temple corridor, it is revered as one of the Char Dhams in India and holds immense spiritual importance for Hindu devotees.

Agnitheertham

A Sacred Seaside Ritual Spot

Located near the famous Ramanathaswamy Temple, Agnitheertham is a sacred beach where thousands of pilgrims take a holy dip. According to legends, it is at this very place that Lord Rama worshipped Lord Shiva after defeating Ravana, and a dip here is said to absolve one of all sins.

12 Jyotirlinga Models

All Sacred Shrines in One Place

The Brahma Kumaris Centre in Rameshwaram features beautifully crafted models of all twelve Jyotirlingas found across India. This unique exhibition offers devotees an opportunity to pay respects to all the lingas in a single sacred location, close to the Ramanathaswamy Temple.

Jada Theertham

Ramayana Heritage Site

Jada Theertham is a serene pond located near the main temple, believed to be the place where Lord Rama and Lord Lakshmana washed their hair before worshipping Lord Shiva. The calm and sacred atmosphere of this theertham makes it a peaceful spot for reflection and prayer.

Five-Faced Hanuman Temple

Divine Power of Panchamukhi Hanuman

The Five-Faced Hanuman Temple, or Panchamukhi Hanuman Temple, depicts Lord Hanuman in his rare five-faced form — symbolizing protection, strength, courage, devotion, and victory. The idols here are believed to hold ancient floating stones from the Ram Setu used by Lord Rama’s army.

Kothandaramar Temple

A Divine Shrine of Lord Rama

Situated near Dhanushkodi, Kothandaramar Temple is believed to be the place where Lord Rama crowned Vibhishana as the king of Lanka. The temple’s isolated location surrounded by sea gives it a mystical and serene ambience, drawing pilgrims from all over the world.

Kalam National Memorial

Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Built by the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), the Kalam National Memorial honors India’s Missile Man and former President, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam. The structure blends Mughal and Indian architectural styles and houses rare photographs, personal belongings, and memorabilia of Dr. Kalam.

Pamban Bridge

Engineering Marvel Over the Sea

The iconic Pamban Bridge connects the Indian mainland with Rameshwaram Island. Once India’s longest sea bridge, it showcases breathtaking views of the blue waters beneath. Watching the bridge open up for ships to pass is a spectacular sight for travelers and engineers alike.

Ram Setu (Adam’s Bridge)

The Legendary Bridge to Lanka

Ram Setu, also known as Adam’s Bridge, is a chain of natural limestone shoals that links Rameshwaram to Sri Lanka. Mythologically, it is believed to have been built by Lord Rama’s Vanara army to rescue Goddess Sita — a symbol of divine engineering and devotion.

Villoondi Theertham

The Holy Well Beside the Sea

Villoondi Theertham, meaning “buried bow,” is a sacred spot where Lord Rama is said to have shot an arrow into the sea to create a freshwater spring for Goddess Sita. The unique freshwater well, located just meters from the salty ocean, continues to amaze visitors to this day.

Thiruppullani

Divine Shore Temple

Located near Rameshwaram, Thiruppullani is a revered Vaishnava temple dedicated to Lord Adi Jagannatha (Vishnu). According to legend, Lord Rama prayed here for guidance before crossing the sea to Lanka. The temple’s seaside setting adds to its spiritual charm and sanctity.

Kunthu Kal Beach

Peaceful Coastal Escape

Kunthu Kal Beach offers a serene environment for those seeking peace away from the crowds. The beach gets its name from the natural rock formations found along its coast, and its shallow waters make it ideal for a calm evening stroll or quiet contemplation.

Jada Tirtham

Holy Pool of Purification

Jada Tirtham is one of the sacred water bodies near Rameshwaram. It is believed that a dip here helps purify the soul and body. Surrounded by lush greenery and ancient shrines, it serves as a tranquil reminder of Rameshwaram’s deep spiritual roots.

Sea World Aquarium

Explore Marine Life Up Close

Sea World Aquarium offers visitors a glimpse into the fascinating underwater world. It showcases a wide variety of colorful fishes, rare marine species, shells, and corals — making it a fun and educational experience for families and children.

Boating Facility

Experience the Sea Adventure

Enjoy a scenic boat ride across the blue waters of Rameshwaram. The boating facility accommodates up to 100 passengers per trip, offering a 30-minute adventure at affordable rates. It’s a refreshing experience for tourists wanting to see the island from the sea.
